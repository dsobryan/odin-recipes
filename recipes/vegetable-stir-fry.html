Vegetable Stir Fry

Description

Vegetable Stir Fry is a quick and easy dish that's packed with flavor and nutrition. This vibrant dish features a variety of colorful vegetables saut√©ed in a savory sauce made from soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and sesame oil. It's perfect for a healthy weeknight dinner and can be customized with your favorite vegetables.

The beauty of a stir fry is its versatility. You can use almost any vegetable you have on hand, making it a great way to clean out the fridge. Common vegetables used include bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, snap peas, and mushrooms. Serve it over rice or noodles for a complete meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • Cooked rice or noodles, for serving
  • Sliced green onions and sesame seeds, for garnish

Method

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Add the bell peppers, broccoli, carrot, snap peas, and mushrooms to the skillet. Stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, and cornstarch mixture. Pour the sauce over the vegetables and stir to coat evenly.
  4. Continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and the vegetables are well coated.
  5. Serve the vegetable stir fry over cooked rice or noodles. Garnish with sliced green onions and sesame seeds. Enjoy!
