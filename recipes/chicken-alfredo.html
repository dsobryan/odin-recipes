Chicken Alfredo

Description

Chicken Alfredo is a rich and creamy pasta dish that has become a favorite comfort food for many. Originating from Rome, this dish combines fettuccine pasta with a creamy Alfredo sauce made from butter, heavy cream, and Parmesan cheese. The addition of tender, pan-seared chicken breasts makes it a hearty and satisfying meal.

The key to a great Chicken Alfredo lies in the sauce, which should be smooth, rich, and cheesy. Freshly grated Parmesan cheese melts into the cream and butter to create a velvety sauce that coats the pasta and chicken perfectly. For an added touch, you can include garlic, black pepper, and a sprinkle of parsley to enhance the flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 pound fettuccine pasta
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish

Method

  1. Season the chicken breasts with salt and black pepper. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until golden brown and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the butter and heavy cream, stirring until the butter is melted and the mixture is well combined.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and gradually whisk in the Parmesan cheese until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Season with additional salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Meanwhile, cook the fettuccine pasta according to package instructions. Drain and add the pasta to the skillet with the Alfredo sauce. Toss to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce.
  5. Slice the cooked chicken and serve on top of the Alfredo pasta. Garnish with chopped parsley and additional Parmesan cheese, if desired. Serve immediately.
