Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that has become a beloved comfort food worldwide. Originating from the Emilia-Romagna region of Italy, lasagna is a hearty casserole that layers wide, flat pasta sheets with a rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and a generous amount of cheese. The key to a great lasagna lies in its sauce, typically a slow-cooked ragu made with ground meat, tomatoes, onions, and garlic, which is simmered to perfection to develop deep, savory flavors. The alternating layers of pasta, sauce, and cheese are baked until bubbly and golden, creating a dish that's both indulgent and satisfying.

Beyond its traditional roots, lasagna has inspired countless variations to suit different tastes and dietary needs. Vegetarian versions often replace the meat with a variety of vegetables like spinach, zucchini, and mushrooms, while still maintaining the essential layers of pasta, tomato sauce, and cheese. There are also lasagnas that use alternative ingredients like gluten-free pasta or dairy-free cheese to accommodate specific dietary restrictions. Whether enjoyed in its classic form or as a creative twist, lasagna remains a versatile and universally loved dish that brings people together around the table.

ingredients

  • 500 grams fresh lasagna pasta sheets
  • 2 cups finely grated parmesan
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 1 medium brown onion,chopped finely
  • i trimmed celery stalk chopped finely
  • 600g minced beef
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 800 grams canned, crushed tomatoes
  • 1 medium carrot, grated finely
  • 1 tsp dried oregano leaves
  • 1.5 cups beef stock
  • 1/2 cup coarsely chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley or basil
  • 125 grams butter
  • 3/4 cupe plain flour
  • 5 cups hot milk

Method

  1. Make bolognese sauce: Heat oil in large heavy-based frying pan over medium heat; cook onion and celery, stirring, for 10 minutes or until vegetables soften. Add beef; cook, breaking up the lumps with a wooden spoon, for 5 minutes or until beef changes colour. Add garlic and tomato paste; cook, stirring, 1 minute. Stir in tomatoes and carrot then oregano and stock; bring to the boil. Reduce heat; simmer, uncovered, for 10 minutes or until thickened slightly. Stir in parsley.
  2. Preheat oven to 200°C/400°F. Grease a deep 25cm x 35cm (10-inch x 14-inch) baking dish.
  3. Make bechamel sauce: Melt butter in a medium saucepan, add flour; stir until mixture forms a smooth paste. Gradually whisk in milk; bring to the boil, whisking, until sauce boils and thickens. Cool.
  4. Spread ½ cup of the white sauce over base of dish. Top with a layer of pasta sheets, a third of the bolognese sauce, ½ cup of the parmesan and 1 cup of the remaining white sauce in dish. Repeat layering two more times, starting with pasta sheets and ending with white sauce. Top lasagne with remaining cheese.
  5. Bake lasagne for 40 minutes or until top is browned lightly. Stand for 15 minutes before cutting.
